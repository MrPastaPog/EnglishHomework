"The Birds" By Aristophanes

"The Birds" by Aristophanes is a comedic play about two Athenians who want to break free from their society and create a better world. The two Athenians Pisthetaerus and Euelpides are sick of the politics, the laws and the corruption within Athens. Aristophanes uses comedies as a tool to make commentary on Athens at the time. This article will explore the themes that Aristophanes used to create the play "The Birds" such as, Freedom, Power and Comedy. The analysis of the characters Pistheraerus, Euelpides, Tereus and the Chorus Leader and how they link to the central themes. Athens and Cloudcuckooland will be examined settings that reflect on the play's themes.
